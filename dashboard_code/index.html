ETF's vs. Mutual Funds

What Is The Difference Between Mutual Funds and ETF's?

ETF's or exchange-traded funds are traded on exchanges by anyone from a regular investor to a fund manager. They are a basket of securities or companies in a certain sector or with certain criteria that allows investors lower risk with more reward over time.

Mutual Funds are business's that manage clients money for the individual investor. These portfolio managers select a combination of compannies to invest in with a goal to have better returns than ETF's and index's, as well as tailor these picks around the investors profile. These benefit comes with a large minimum deposit and a certain holding period of clients money. Mutual Funds spend a lot of money on analyst, economic and industry research, as well as CEO meetings. These are some reasons that make Mutual Funds very lucrative.

Difference Between Growth, Value, and Blend Strategies

Growth investing is focused on young or small companies who have expected earnings growth and are expected to grow at an above-average rate compared to their industry sector. Growth investors look at companies poised to expand and increase profitability potential in the future.

Value investing is involved with picking stocks that are trading for less value than what is on paper, basically the under-valued. Value investors tend to focus on financial analysis as the foundation of their conclusions.

Blend investing is intuitive in it's name, they are a category that invest in both growth and value stocks. the goal behind a blend strategy is to have a diversified portfolio that takes advantage of the capital gains potential of the growth segment and dividend income of the value segment.
